# Anti-patterns

Use this file for the final cleanup pass or when a draft still feels synthetic after a normal rewrite.

## 1. Generic thesis opener

Problem:
- Opens with a broad universal statement that could fit almost any essay.

Fix:
- Start from the user's actual observation, memory, disagreement, or mechanism.

## 2. Pre-packaged contrast

Problem:
- Uses lines like "That doesn't mean X" or "This is not just Y. It is Z." too neatly and too often.

Fix:
- State the claim directly, or replace the contrast with the concrete reason.

## 3. Inflated significance

Problem:
- Tells the reader something "marks a pivotal moment," "reflects a broader shift," or "underscores the importance" without proving it.

Fix:
- Say what happened and why it matters in this case.

## 4. Abstract nouns doing all the work

Problem:
- Leans on words like landscape, transformation, innovation, significance, capability, optimization.

Fix:
- Replace with actors, actions, systems, costs, constraints, and examples.

## 5. Vague authority

Problem:
- Says experts, observers, critics, reports, or many people without grounding.

Fix:
- Name the source, or remove the attribution.

## 6. Fake depth from trailing -ing phrases

Problem:
- Adds clauses like highlighting, underscoring, reflecting, fostering, symbolizing to create synthetic depth.

Fix:
- End the sentence earlier or replace with a specific factual claim.

## 7. AI vocabulary cluster

Words to cut or challenge:
- leverage
- robust
- seamless
- pivotal
- underscore
- tapestry
- intricate
- landscape
- crucial
- showcase
- enhance

Fix:
- Prefer plain words unless the technical term is actually needed.

## 8. Rule-of-three symmetry

Problem:
- Forces ideas into polished trios too often.

Fix:
- Use the number of items the thought actually needs.

## 9. Elegant variation

Problem:
- Swaps in synonyms just to avoid repetition.

Fix:
- Repeat the right noun if it keeps the prose clearer.

## 10. Emphasis formatting overuse

Problem:
- Uses too many em dashes, bold phrases, inline headers, or title-cased headings to simulate punch.

Fix:
- Use punctuation and formatting only when it clarifies structure.

## 11. Soulless neutrality

Problem:
- Reports ideas cleanly but gives no sense that anyone actually believes, doubts, or cares about them.

Fix:
- Add the writer's stake, hesitation, irritation, curiosity, or conviction when the source supports it.

## 12. Formula ending

Problem:
- Ends with a neat moral, tidy recap, or TED-talk sentence.

Fix:
- End on the sharpest remaining implication, unresolved tension, or concrete takeaway.
